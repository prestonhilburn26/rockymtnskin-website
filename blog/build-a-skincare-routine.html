Affordable Skincare in SLC: How to Build a Routine That Actually Works

By Jake Howard, Licensed Master Esthetician  •  June 20, 2025

Let’s talk about something most people are too scared to say out loud:

Skincare is expensive—or at least, it can be.

If your entire For You Page is influencers pushing $200 serums, medical spas upselling you into memberships, or brand reps acting like a $300 moisturizer is “essential,” of course it feels out of reach. And if you’ve ever left a facial feeling like you needed to refinance your car to afford the retail shelf… yeah, I get it.

But here’s the truth, and it’s the backbone of everything I do at Rocky Mountain Skin:

You don’t need a luxury budget.

You need a curated plan.

Not curated by TikTok.

Not curated by Sephora sales goals.

Curated by someone who actually listens to you, looks at your skin, understands your goals, and respects your budget.

That’s my job.

Why Skincare Feels Complicated (And Why It Doesn’t Have to Be)

Most people come to me overwhelmed. They’ve tried a lot of things, followed every trend, mixed too many active ingredients, or bought a routine that was way too strong for their skin.

It’s not your fault. The industry is noisy on purpose.

My approach is the opposite: keep it simple, keep it strategic, and build up slowly.

You don’t start a workout routine with an elite-level training session.

You don’t start skincare with the strongest products on the shelf, either.

Your skin has to be trained.

When the barrier is healthy, hydrated, and functioning, it can handle stronger ingredients, actives work better, and treatments make a bigger impact. When we skip that step, things fall apart fast.

What a Curated Routine Actually Means

A curated routine isn’t about expensive products—it’s about using the right products in the right order at the right time.

For some people, that means:

a Target cleanser

a simple moisturizer

a mineral sunscreen that doesn’t sting

one solid serum

maybe a retinol when the skin is ready

For others, it means:

a PCA Skin retinol

an Image antioxidant

a pro treatment plan

a seasonal “skin training” upgrade

Everything has its place. Not everyone needs the most advanced formula on day one. Not everyone needs a $120 serum. But if a professional-grade product will save you time, irritation, or frustration—and your skin is ready for it—I’ll point you toward it.

Where to Save and Where to Splurge

This is my general rule of thumb:

Save on:

cleansers

basic moisturizers

hydrating toners

simple SPFs

Spend on:

retinol

mineral sunscreen (if your skin is picky)

certain vitamin C formulas

corrective serums that genuinely move the needle

Split the difference on:

exfoliants

pigmentation serums

redness or sensitivity treatments

A $15 cleanser can perform just as well as a $40 one.

A $15 vitamin C usually cannot.

I’ll tell you the difference.

Who I Work With

The people in my treatment room aren’t influencers or celebrities.

They’re real humans:

teens with acne

moms whose skin barrier is wrecked from years of harsh actives

teachers and nurses with dehydrated, overworked skin

queer clients dealing with hormone shifts

bartenders, servers, Lyft drivers

students who need routines that don’t eat their whole paycheck

Everyone deserves good skin.

Everyone deserves guidance that respects their life—not just their wallet.
