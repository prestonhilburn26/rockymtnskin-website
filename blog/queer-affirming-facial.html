What It’s Like to Get a Facial in a Queer-Affirming Studio

By Jake Howard, LME

Published: November 2024

You know that feeling when you walk into a spa and instantly wonder: “Am I going to feel safe here — accepted here? Will I have to explain who I am, how I identify, or hope they just get it?” At a queer-affirming studio, that tension doesn’t exist. Instead, you walk in, and the space already understands that people are complex, bodies and identities diverse, and skin is skin — deserving of respect, care, and dignity.

Walking in: no judgement, just welcome

From the moment you arrive, the vibe is different. You’re not treated like a box to check. There’s no assumption about who you are, what your body looks like, or how you identify. Instead, you’re met with openness, inclusivity, and real intention — honoring your pronouns, your identity, and your story without insistence or pressure.

Treatment with respect — skin-first, identity-affirming

Facials in this environment aren’t just about skin: they’re about care, trust, and comfort. The focus is on you — your skin needs, personal preferences, and comfort levels. That means:

Clear, respectful intake — you’re invited to share pronouns or chosen name if you like, but it’s optional.

Safe, tailored skincare with no assumptions about gender norms.

A recognition that identity, stress, and lived experience impact skin — and treatment should honor that.

Healing more than skin

Getting a facial in an affirming studio isn’t just a skincare appointment — it’s a chance to rest, be seen, and feel safe in your body. For many, especially those who have felt marginalized in typical spas or med-spaces, this can feel deeply healing.

Why this matters

Because skin is more than skin. For people who are queer, trans, nonbinary, or outside traditional beauty norms, a spa that sees you and honors you offers more than results — it offers belonging.
